32 /*!***************************************************************************
33  * @brief Interrupt Latency During Flash Operations
34  *
35  * When writing to or erasing flash, interrupts must be disabled to avoid
36  * executing code in flash while the flash is being reprogrammed. This
37  * constraint is met internally by the driver. User code does not need
38  * to safeguard against this.
39  *
40  * Additionally, to avoid extremely large interrupt latencies that would be
41  * incurred if entire blocks were written with interrupts disabled, block
42  * writes to flash are broken into multiple smaller sizes.
43  *
44  * Even with this scheme in place, latencies of roughly 64 microseconds will
45  * be incurred while flash is being written to.
46  *
47  * A similar caveat applies to flash erase operations. Erasing an entire
48  * flash sector (the minimal amount that can be erased at a time) can take
49  * roughly 8 milliseconds. This entire operation must be performed with
50  * interrupts disabled. Here again, this requirement is met internally
51  * by the driver and flash region erases are performed one sector at a
52  * time to minimize this significant latency.impact.
53  *
54  * Care must be taken by the user to not perform flash write or erase
55  * operations during latency critical phases of an application.
56  *
57  *****************************************************************************
58  */
